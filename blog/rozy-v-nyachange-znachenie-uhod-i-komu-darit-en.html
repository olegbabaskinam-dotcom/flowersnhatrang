Roses in Nha Trang: Prices, Meaning, How Many to Give and Care

The rose is the most universal flower there is: people give it for birthdays, anniversaries, as a token of love and simply for no reason at all. But behind a beautiful bouquet hides a whole language — colour, variety and the number of roses can say more than any card. And in Nha Trang there is one extra factor: the tropical heat, in which a bouquet must not only be arranged beautifully but also delivered fresh. In this big guide we break it all down — how much roses cost in Nha Trang, what their colours mean, how many to give, which variety to choose and how to make a bouquet last.

What the colour of roses means

A rose's colour is its main message. By choosing a shade you speak about your feelings in advance, even without a single word:

  • ❤️ Red — love and passion. The classic for a partner, a confession or a romantic surprise.
  • 🤍 White — purity, respect and admiration. A universal, elegant choice for any occasion.
  • 💗 Pink — tenderness, gratitude and care. Perfect for a mum, a friend or the start of a relationship.
  • 💛 Yellow — joy, friendship and warmth. The old myth that yellow means separation is just that — a myth.
  • 🧡 Orange — energy and admiration, a bright wish of happiness.
  • 🌈 Mixed — a lively, festive bouquet for those who love colour.

How many roses to give: the language of numbers

In the tradition of giving flowers, parity matters as much as the count. In Russia, Vietnam and Korea a live bouquet is given with an odd number of flowers — an even number is reserved for funeral ceremonies. The exception is large bouquets from 50 stems and up: there the flowers are no longer counted, and the bouquet reads as one whole.

  • 🌹 Up to 11 roses — give an odd number: 5, 7, 9, 11.
  • 💐 From 25 roses — the count no longer matters, what counts is the volume.
  • 👑 Anniversary or proposal — a large 51- or 101-rose bouquet, basket or box.

Rose varieties and which to choose

"Rose" really means dozens of different flowers, and the variety decides how the bouquet looks and what it costs. Here is what appears most often in our bouquets:

  • 🌸 Peony roses — lush, densely petalled, similar to peonies. The most romantic and photogenic, ideal for a partner.
  • 🌹 Classic roses — the familiar elegant shape, a safe choice for any occasion.
  • 🌷 Ecuadorian roses — tall stems and large heads, they look premium and last well.
  • 🪴 Spray roses — many small blooms on one stem, great for a light, airy mix.

Who to give roses to and for what occasion

The beauty of roses is that they suit almost everyone — only the colour, variety and size change:

  • 💞 Wife or girlfriend — red or white-pink peony roses for an anniversary, birthday or confession.
  • 🌷 Mum — soft pink or white roses as a sign of warmth and gratitude.
  • 🤝 Colleague — a restrained neutral bouquet, medium size, without excess romance.
  • 🎓 Teacher — a classic bright bouquet, traditionally given for the start of the school year and holidays.
  • 🎂 Birthday — any colour the person loves; a mix or peony roses work beautifully.

  • 💘 14 February, Valentine's Day — the peak of romantic orders, red roses sell out first.
  • 🌸 8 March, Women's Day — a huge day for flowers among Russian-speaking and Vietnamese customers.

Roses in Vietnamese and Korean traditions

Nha Trang is an international city, and understanding local traditions helps you avoid a misstep with a gift. In Vietnam roses are firmly tied to romance: they are given especially on 14 February and on International Women's Day on 8 March, and the red rose remains the main symbol of love. Vietnamese culture also pays attention to numbers and avoids even counts in living bouquets, just like the Russian tradition.

In Korea flowers are a frequent and meaningful gift — for graduations, confessions and anniversaries, often with a heartfelt card. Koreans love delicate, well-styled bouquets and pastel shades. Knowing this, we help our Korean guests choose an arrangement that feels right for the occasion.

How to keep roses fresh in Nha Trang's heat

The hot, humid climate is the main enemy of cut flowers. But with simple care, roses easily last several days even in the middle of a holiday:

  • ✂️ Trim the stems at an angle by 1–2 cm under running water before putting them in a vase — the flower starts "drinking" again.
  • 💧 Change the water every day and keep the vase out of direct sun and away from the air-conditioner flow.
  • 🍌 Keep the bouquet away from fruit — it speeds up wilting.
  • 🏨 In a hotel room, the coolest spot at night helps the roses hold longer.
